Official League Rulebook
Guidelines and standards for a fair, competitive, and enjoyable experience in EGL league matches and administration.
In-Game Rules
1.1 Hosts & Halftime ▾

Each team is allowed to host one half of the match in their region. Failure to join the other team's host will result in an automatic loss.

During the second half, if your team has not hosted yet, you may request to switch to your host. If no request is made, continuing to play in the same host will be considered acceptance of that host, and the half will not be replayed.

If the match proceeds to extra time (tournament matches), the first half of extra time will be played in the same host as the most recent half. The second half of extra time may be switched to the other team's host if they request it.

Penalty: Auto loss or voided match
Appeal: No (Host decisions must be made during the match)
1.2 Mercy Rule ▾

The mercy rule enforces the game to be stopped after a certain goal difference is achieved. A team managing a goal difference of 6 goals will automatically win, and the game will be stopped regardless of the leg.

For example, if the score reaches 6-0, the team who scored 6 goals will automatically win, and the game will be stopped regardless of the leg.

Result: Automatic win declared at 6-goal difference
1.3 Pass Back (PB) ▾

Intentional pass-backs to the goalkeeper from open play are not permitted. The goalkeeper must not pick up a deliberate pass-back from a teammate with their hands.

Penalty: Free kick awarded to opposing team
Repeat offence: Yellow card
1.4 Last Centerback ▾

If your last centerback receives a red card, you may request a temporary substitution to maintain defensive shape. This must be agreed upon before the match or declared within 2 minutes of the incident.

Appeal: Yes — within 2 minutes of the incident
1.5Time Wasting▾

Deliberate time wasting in any form — including holding the ball excessively in the goalkeeper's hands, taking unusually long goal kicks, or stalling restarts — is prohibited.

Penalty: Yellow card; repeated offences result in a red card
1.6Unstable WiFi▾

If a player disconnects due to unstable WiFi, the team may pause to allow the player to reconnect. A maximum of 5 minutes is allowed for reconnection. Persistent disconnections may result in a forfeit.

Grace period: 5 minutes to reconnect
Failure to reconnect: Match continues without the player or result stands
1.7Fielding Illegal Players▾

Teams must only field registered players. Using unregistered or banned players in a match is strictly prohibited and will result in severe penalties for the club.

Penalty: Match forfeit (0-3 loss) + potential points deduction
1.8Ragdoll Save▾

Intentionally using ragdoll physics to save or block shots is considered exploiting the game mechanics and is not permitted.

Penalty: Goal awarded + yellow card
1.9Last Man▾

If only one outfield player remains due to disconnections, the match will be paused and the team will be given 3 minutes to reconnect additional players before the match is either continued or forfeited.

1.10Spacing / Corner Rushing▾

Players must maintain fair spacing during set pieces. Rushing the corner taker or crowding the penalty area before a corner kick is taken is prohibited.

Penalty: Retaken corner or free kick
1.11Intentional Fouls▾

Deliberately fouling a player to stop a goal-scoring opportunity or break up play repeatedly will be penalised. This includes slide tackle spam or body check abuse.

Penalty: Yellow or red card at referee's discretion
1.12Third-Party Software▾

The use of any third-party software, macros, scripts, or modifications that provide an unfair advantage is strictly forbidden and constitutes cheating.

Penalty: Immediate ban from the league
1.13Exploitation During Matches▾

Exploiting game bugs, glitches, or unintended mechanics during a match is not permitted. This includes but is not limited to wall clipping, physics abuse, or animation cancelling for competitive advantage.

Penalty: Match voided or result reversed + suspension
1.14Delay▾

Teams must be ready to start within 10 minutes of the scheduled kick-off time. Delays beyond this window without prior communication will result in a forfeit.

Penalty: Forfeit after 10 minutes of delay
1.15Red Cards▾

A player who receives a red card during a match is suspended for the following league match. Two yellow cards in the same match also result in a red card and the same suspension applies.

Suspension: 1 match ban (minimum)
1.16In-Game Glitching▾

Using known in-game glitches to gain an unfair advantage — such as going through walls, invisible player exploits, or animation-lock abuse — is a bannable offence.

Penalty: Match void + 2-match ban
1.17Kick-Offs▾

At kick-off, all players must be in their respective halves. The ball must be touched by two players before it can leave the centre circle. Kick-off goals are not counted unless the ball has been touched by two players first.

1.18Trolling / Sabotage▾

Any player found deliberately sabotaging their own team, trolling opponents, using offensive language in match, or intentionally causing disruption will be penalised.

Penalty: Instant removal from match + suspension review
1.19Knockout Games▾

Knockout matches that end in a draw after 90 minutes will proceed to extra time (2x10 minutes). If still level, a penalty shootout will decide the winner. Standard shootout rules apply (5 kicks each, then sudden death).

1.20Side Net▾

Goals that enter via the side netting are not counted. If there is visible proof a ball entered via the side net, the goal will be disallowed and play restarted with a goal kick.

Appeal: Yes — video proof required within 5 minutes
1.21Penalties▾

Penalty kicks during normal play follow standard rules. The goalkeeper must remain on the goal line until the ball is kicked. The taker cannot touch the ball again until another player has touched it.

League Rules
2.1Jailing▾

A player who goes absent without notice for more than 3 scheduled match weeks will be jailed — meaning frozen from all transfer activity and placed on a watchlist. Jailed players cannot play in official matches until reinstated.

Condition: 3+ weeks absent without notification
Resolution: Contact admin to be reinstated
2.2Emergency Signings▾

If a team has fewer than 5 available players for a scheduled match, they may request an emergency signing. The signed player must be a free agent and the request must be made at least 2 hours before kick-off.

Limit: 1 emergency signing per matchday
Window: Must be done 2+ hours before kick-off
2.3Hosts▾

The home team is responsible for setting up the match server for the first half. Both teams must agree on the host region. If no agreement is reached, the league admin will assign a neutral host.

2.4Substitutions▾

Each team is allowed up to 5 substitutions per match. Substitutions can only be made during a stoppage in play. The substitute must be registered in the team's squad before the match begins.

Max substitutions: 5 per match
2.5Manager Activity▾

Managers are required to be active and available for all official matchdays. A manager who misses two or more consecutive match weeks without notifying the league admin may be replaced.

Consequence: Manager removal after 2 missed matchdays without notice
2.6Transfer Rules▾

All transfers must be processed through the official transfer portal. Players cannot be transferred mid-matchweek. A player cannot play for their new club in the same matchday their transfer was processed.

Window: Between matchdays only
Breach: Transfer invalidated + points deduction
2.7Arriving at Matches▾

All players must be in the lobby and ready at least 5 minutes before kick-off. The match can begin when both teams have a minimum of 5 outfield players + 1 goalkeeper present and ready.

Minimum: 5 outfield + 1 GK to start
Failure: Forfeit after 10-minute grace period
2.8Stream / Referee▾

A match cannot be played until all of the following are confirmed and present:

Required: A referee must be present before kick-off
Required: A streamer must be present before kick-off
Required: Both managers must be present before kick-off

If any of the above conditions are not met at kick-off time, the match must be delayed until they are fulfilled or rescheduled via the postponement process.

2.9Postponing (PP)▾

Matches may be postponed or preponed within a window of 3 hours to 1 day before or after the originally scheduled kick-off time.

Both preponing and postponing require agreement from both managers before the change is confirmed. A manager cannot request a postponement without providing a valid reason to the league admin.

Window: 3 hours – 1 day before or after scheduled time
Requirement: Mutual agreement from both managers
Invalid request: No valid reason = request denied
2.10Competitive Integrity▾

To maintain the competitive standard of the league, a team cannot lose 2 or more matches by the mercy rule within a single season.

Teams that accumulate multiple mercy rule losses may be reviewed and subject to disbandment at the discretion of league administration.

Limit: Maximum 1 mercy rule loss per season
Consequence: 2+ mercy losses may result in team disbandment
2.11Note on Evidence▾

Any evidence submitted in connection with a report, dispute, or appeal must be no older than 14 days (2 weeks) from the date of the incident.

This rule exists to prevent blackmail, undue pressure, and the use of outdated material to unfairly target individuals within the league community.

Admissibility limit: 14 days from incident date
Reason: Prevents blackmail and undue pressure on individuals
2.12Self Pass▾

A self pass occurs when a player throws or kicks the ball directly to themselves without another player touching it in between.

If a self pass is committed, it will result in a foul being called and a free kick awarded to the opposition from the spot of the infringement.

Penalty: Foul called + free kick awarded to opposition
2.13Loans▾

A loan occurs when a team fields a player who is not officially signed to their squad. Using loaned players without proper registration is a violation of squad rules.

If a team fields 2 or more unregistered loan players in a single match, it will result in an immediate forfeit (FFT) for that team.

1 loan player: Warning issued
2+ loan players: Immediate forfeit (FFT)
2.14Lineups▾

Both teams are required to submit their starting lineups between 30 and 20 minutes before kick-off. Lineups must be sent to the designated channel or referee before this window closes.

If one or both teams fail to submit their lineup within the required timeframe, the offending team(s) will be considered to have forfeited the match immediately.

Window: 30 – 20 minutes before kick-off
Late or missing lineup: Immediate FFT (forfeit)
2.15Cheating▾

Any player or team caught cheating during a match will face immediate consequences. Cheating includes but is not limited to:

• Anchoring the ball (:a) multiple times deliberately
• Manipulating or falsifying the scoreline
• Any other form of match manipulation
Punishment: Automatic win awarded to the opposition
Severe cases: League ban under 1.12 (Third-Party Software)
2.16Servers▾

All server types are permitted for use in matches with the exception of those listed below. Using a banned server type will result in the match being voided or forfeited.

If the opposition team selects the same PST server again after already using it, it is considered an immediate automatic forfeit (FFT) for that team.

Banned server type: PST Servers (repeated use by same team)
Note: Additional banned server types will be announced as the season progresses
Punishment: Immediate auto FFT (forfeit) for the offending team
